Tracking case study

Building revenue tracking for a gym without a website

Offline service businessEgyptName withheld

When I started with this business, there was no website, no checkout, and no clean purchase event to read. Sales were happening through messages, WhatsApp, and offline follow-up. I couldn't judge performance from leads only, because a lead means very little if it does not become a paid subscription.

Context

Not a traditional e-commerce case

Industry

Gym and fitness subscriptions.

Starting point

The gym had reached a certain monthly sales ceiling before, but it was not repeating that level consistently.

Main challenge

The business needed offer thinking and a simple sales tracking system that could show which conversations, packages, and follow-ups were turning into collected revenue.

Problem

The missing revenue link

No website tracking

There was no automated purchase path, so the usual e-commerce signals were not available.

Lead-to-sale visibility

The first problem was connecting messages and WhatsApp conversations to actual memberships, package sales, and revenue collected.

Offer complexity

Different subscriptions and packages needed to be tracked separately, otherwise the team could not see which offer was doing the work.

Operational constraints

The setup had to be practical for the way the gym already sold. A complex tech stack would have slowed the team down.

My role

What I made visible

Sales tracking sheet

I built a Google Sheets tracker for subscriptions, package sales, revenue, monthly targets, and actual sales performance.

Offer tracking

I connected each package to sales and revenue so the business could see which offer was helping the target and which one was only creating conversations.

Revenue view

I shifted the discussion from “how many leads did we get?” to “how much revenue did this activity create?”

Monthly target logic

I added target logic so the team could see the gap between current revenue, monthly target, and the daily sales pace needed to close it.

Growth logic

The measurement logic

For a gym like this, tracking had to start after the message. I needed to connect marketing activity to follow-up, memberships, packages, and paid revenue, so the business could manage performance without needing a website first.

Takeaway

Revenue became trackable without a website

The strongest part was not the sheet itself. It was giving the business a way to see sales performance clearly without a website or heavy technical setup. Leads, packages, memberships, and revenue finally sat in the same operating view.
