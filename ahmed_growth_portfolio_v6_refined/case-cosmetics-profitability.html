Profitability case study

From break-even pressure to profitable acquisition

CosmeticsEgyptConfidential client

When I started, the brand was fighting in a crowded cosmetics category and the account was sitting too close to break-even. I did not treat the brief as “increase ROAS” only. I needed to find which structure, creative angles, and ad sets were actually worth trusting with more spend.

Context

The pressure inside the account

Industry

Cosmetics and beauty products.

Starting point

The brand had demand, but the economics were tight. The account needed stronger acquisition efficiency before scaling could feel comfortable.

Main challenge

What I cared about first was separating real signal from noise: which campaigns were readable, which creatives had buying intent, and which results could repeat.

Problem

What made profitability hard to read

ROAS pressure

The account was hovering near break-even, so weak tests could not be allowed to sit for too long.

Creative direction

The brand needed sharper testing around UGC, product angles, and reasons to buy beyond the product being available.

Campaign structure

The Meta setup needed a cleaner shape so I could see which ad sets deserved attention and which ones were hiding weak signal.

Budget inconsistency

The operating window was short and not perfectly stable, so I had to extract clarity quickly while the account was active.

My role

How I cleaned up the acquisition read

Campaign restructuring

I rebuilt the Meta campaign structure so testing, spend, CPA, ROAS, and purchase volume were easier to compare.

Creative testing

I focused on product and UGC angles that could create stronger buying intent, not just more impressions.

Audience logic

I improved how audience tests and broad or Advantage-style signals were interpreted, so the account was not judged from one surface metric.

Performance interpretation

I kept the attention on ad sets and creatives that showed healthier CPA and ROAS together, because one metric alone can mislead in a tight-margin account.

Growth logic

The profitability logic

In cosmetics, the difference between a weak account and a profitable one is often the quality of interpretation. I needed to know which product stories, UGC angles, and campaign signals were strong enough to scale, and which ones were only creating noise.

Takeaway

Profitability came from better reading
