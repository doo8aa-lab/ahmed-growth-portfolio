Scaling case study

Turning failed website attempts into a Ramadan growth engine

F&B e-commerceEgyptName withheld

At the start, the products made sense for Ramadan: dates, honey, nuts, olive oil, and seasonal food bundles. The problem was not demand. The business had tried website sales before, but the results were unstable and break-even performance was not reliable enough for a short season.

Context

What the season required

Industry

Dates, honey, nuts, olive oil, and Ramadan-related F&B products.

Starting point

The client already had products people could buy during Ramadan. What was missing was a website purchase system that could read performance clearly and keep decisions moving every day.

Timing

Ramadan does not give you a long learning curve. I needed to understand the offers, the buying behavior, and the operational limits quickly enough to protect the season.

Problem

Why the previous attempts were fragile

No stable website funnel

The website path was not yet consistent enough from a CPP, ROAS, and purchase-quality point of view. Traffic was not the only issue.

No tracking foundation

The business needed cleaner tracking and a reporting rhythm to separate real winners from temporary noise.

Offers were scattered

There were several product and bundle directions live at the same time. Not all of them were under my full control, so I had to read the market response fast.

Operational pressure

Seasonal F&B is unforgiving. If the team waits too long to see what is working, the best days of the season are already gone.

My role

How I made the season easier to read

E-commerce growth system

I organized the work around website purchases, not just campaign activity, so the business could judge growth by orders and revenue.

Meta restructuring

I rebuilt the Meta structure and campaign logic so testing, budget movement, and winning directions were easier to separate.

Full-funnel setup

I created a clearer website purchase funnel across acquisition, offer testing, retargeting, and conversion.

Content system

I focused content around the products and bundles with the strongest Ramadan intent, then kept the weak angles from eating the season.

Reporting system

I set a reporting rhythm that made daily decisions faster: what to push, what to pause, and where performance was actually coming from.

Offer filtering

Because offer control was not always clean, I focused on identifying the winners quickly and cutting weak directions before they consumed too much time or spend.

Growth logic

What had to become controlled

For this kind of seasonal push, clarity is the asset. I needed the team to see which products, offers, creatives, and funnel steps deserved attention while there was still time to act. The goal was to turn Ramadan from a risky rush into a more controlled growth engine.

Takeaway

The real win was seasonal clarity

The numbers improved, but the stronger result was the decision system. The business could see what was moving, act faster, and run Ramadan with more control instead of treating the season like one high-pressure bet.
