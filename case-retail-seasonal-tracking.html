Operations case study

Creating multi-branch visibility for a seasonal retail push

Retail / multi-branchEgyptPrivate client

When I looked at the season, the problem was not only marketing. The business had branches, a sales target, and a short window where every day mattered. If management discovered the gap after the season ended, there would be no time left to fix it.

Context

A seasonal operations problem

Industry

Retail / seasonal sales.

Starting point

This was not a normal ads case. The business needed to follow branch sales during a concentrated seasonal push and know where the target stood every day.

Main challenge

I needed management to see which branches were ahead, which branches were behind, and how much sales volume was still required to finish the season properly.

Problem

What management could not see fast enough

Branch visibility

Sales were coming from different branches, but the picture was not quick enough for daily action.

Seasonal pressure

The risk was simple: find the gap at the end, and the opportunity to correct it is already gone.

Target tracking

The business needed a live view of total sales, achievement rate, remaining target, and daily sales required.

Operational accountability

I wanted the team to know where attention was needed: the strongest branches, the weakest branches, and the locations falling behind target.

My role

What I built for the season

Seasonal tracking dashboard

I built a dashboard for total sales, target, achievement percentage, best branch, weakest branch, average daily sales, and remaining required sales.

Branch-level tracker

I built branch-level tracking for sales, targets, achievement rate, best days, and days above or below target.

Daily decision view

I turned scattered branch sales into one operating view so the team could act during the season, not after it was already over.

Performance accountability

I made it easier for management to decide where to push, where to investigate, and which branches needed support.

Growth logic

The operating logic

Growth work is not only media buying. In a seasonal retail push, sales tracking, branch visibility, and decision systems can decide whether the business corrects course in time or only understands the problem after the window closes.

Visuals

The important views were the ones management could use quickly: target progress, total sales, remaining requirement, and branch-by-branch performance.

Takeaway

The business could act before the season closed

The value was not only knowing the final number. It was giving management a daily view of progress, weak points, and required sales while there was still time to respond.
