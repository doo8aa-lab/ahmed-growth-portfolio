The story behind the work

I Started With Traffic.
Then I Learned Business.

This is not a CV page. It is the journey from campaign execution to business-level growth architecture — told as the progression that shaped how I think.

Performance changed when I saw the business from the inside.

I don't think like someone who only opens Ads Manager. I think from the business side too: pricing, returns, fulfillment, content, sales teams, reporting, and the owner's need to know when growth is actually profitable.

The arc is simple: I ran ads → I built brands → I understood business → I built systems → I now think like a growth company.

The journey

Chapters, not job titles

2017

Affiliate marketing taught me traffic and offer logic.

I started with traffic, funnels, ROI, and the pressure of making spend return more than it costs. This was the first time I understood that ads are only one piece of a larger system.

2019

Media buying made the gap visible.

I became a full media buyer and ran campaigns across industries. The deeper I went, the clearer it became: ad performance and business performance are not always the same thing.

2021

Building my own brand changed how I see every client.

I built and operated my own accessories brand. Pricing, content, fulfillment, returns, and customer pressure stopped being theory. It made me understand what it feels like to be on the client side — and that changed how I evaluate every campaign.

2022

Creative became targeting.

I expanded into content strategy and community building. The lesson was direct: creative is not decoration. It shapes the audience, the intent, and the conversion path before the click happens.

2024

Performance became infrastructure.

Senior e-commerce growth work pushed me into tracking, funnel architecture, decision models, and systems. This is where my work started becoming repeatable across industries.

2025+

Systems became the product.

I started building performance team structures, Creative OS, Performance OS, and decision frameworks. The story is still being written now.

Philosophy

Four beliefs behind the work

Performance is a system.

A result is only valuable when the system behind it can repeat and improve it.

Revenue beats vanity metrics.

Leads, clicks, and ROAS matter only when they connect to contribution and profit.

Offline deserves clean data.

Revenue tracking for businesses without a website can still be disciplined, useful, and fast.

Creative is operational.

Winning creative is not luck. It needs briefs, testing, feedback, and documentation.
