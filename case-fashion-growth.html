Scaling case study

From message-based selling to a scalable Shopify growth system

Fashion e-commerceEgyptConfidential client

When I came in, the business was already selling online and had real demand. The problem was that growth still depended heavily on messages, manual handling, and scattered decisions. Ads alone could not fix that. The business needed an e-commerce system that could carry scale.

Context

What I walked into

Industry

Fashion, Egypt market.

Starting point

The brand had been selling online for years, mostly through Facebook and direct messages. It had product momentum, but daily volume was still under 50 orders and every push created more pressure on the team.

Core risk

If we simply increased spend, the operation would feel the pressure before the business felt the scale. More traffic needed to become trackable purchases, not just more conversations.

Problem

What was limiting scale

No tracking layer

The first thing I noticed was the missing connection between traffic, funnel behavior, purchases, and revenue decisions. Without that layer, scaling would be guesswork.

No full funnel

Acquisition, retargeting, conversion, and retention were not working as one system. Campaigns existed, but the buying journey was not controlled enough.

Scaling ceiling

Message-based selling had carried the business for years, but it was becoming the ceiling. Higher spend would have created more manual follow-up, not a cleaner path to revenue.

Product dependency

The business leaned too much on new launches. I needed to build depth around several winning products so growth did not depend on one product spike at a time.

Pricing and offer instability

Pricing, delivery, returns, and cumulative offers all needed clearer rules. Small decisions there can quietly protect or damage margin at scale.

My role

What I built into the business

Shopify growth infrastructure

I moved the business toward Shopify-based selling so orders, sessions, conversion rate, products, and revenue could be managed from a real e-commerce base.

Full-funnel acquisition system

I rebuilt Meta, TikTok, and Google activity around a full funnel: acquisition, testing, retargeting, conversion, and repeat buying instead of isolated campaign moves.

Content system

I shaped the content direction around product depth, buying triggers, creative testing, and the need to keep more than one product performing at the same time.

Reporting system

I built reporting inside and outside the ad dashboards so decisions were based on revenue, orders, CPA, ROAS, MER, product movement, and operational capacity.

Product and stock strategy

I pushed the business away from one-winner dependency and toward a portfolio of products that could carry volume together.

Offer and pricing logic

I worked on cumulative offers, pricing logic, delivery impact, returns pressure, and AOV so scale would not come at the expense of economics.

CRO and leakage control

I worked on conversion rate, checkout behavior, funnel leakage, and discount logic for customers who already showed strong buying intent.

Growth logic

The logic behind the build

I was not trying to make the account spend more for the sake of spend. I was trying to make higher daily spend less fragile. That meant traffic, content, offers, product depth, pricing, checkout behavior, reporting, and operations had to support each other before the budget moved aggressively.

Takeaway

The stronger result was control

The 107M+ EGP revenue matters, but the more important part is the system behind it. The business became better prepared for higher order volume, higher daily spend, and faster decisions without depending on manual selling to hold everything together.
